Umberto Eco's distinction: you can use a book or you can interpret it. Using a book means taking from it whatever confirms what you already think. Interpreting it means reading it fully and trying to understand what it actually says, on its own terms.

Some books are worth the second kind of attention. They are rare — maybe a few times in a life. With those, the reader's job is not to invent a version of the book that suits them, but to follow the book where it goes.

Reading is always partly invention. But certain books demand fidelity.
