We exist first, then define ourselves through our actions. There is no predetermined nature or divine essence that decides what we are before we live. Our choices are what constitute us.

The genius of Proust is the work of Proust — not what he might have written, but what he actually produced. A project not completed is only an aborted dream. What counts is the reality.

La mauvaise foi (bad faith) is the refusal of this: denying one's freedom, hiding behind excuses, refusing to assume responsibility for one's existence.

Sartre's existentialism is a philosophy of action and construction. Because there is no predetermined essence, every action we take engages not just ourselves but humanity — each choice is, in a sense, an original act that proposes how humans should be.

The feeling is built through action over time. Sentiment is not prior to action; it is proved by it.

Source: notes from memory of reading *L'existentialisme est un humanisme* (last read 2016).
