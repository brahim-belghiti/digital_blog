No matter how we try to escape social categories, we find ourselves positioned within them. There is already so much accumulated in human history — lifestyles, belief systems, identities — that to exist is to find a place within that diaspora of ideas. Every position you take carries a perceived social meaning, a position within some group, a way of being seen in relation to dominant power structures.

At the personal level: we live our subjective experience both as a subject and as an object for other subjects. Simone de Beauvoir's formulation — l'autre c'est l'enfer — is not that other people are enemies, but that the look of the other constitutes us as objects in a world we do not fully control.

In Morocco specifically: dominant belief systems — religious, traditional, economic — determine how certain identities are read. Being positioned outside them is not neutral; it places you.

This does not mean freedom is impossible. It means freedom is not the absence of social constraint — it is what you build within it.
