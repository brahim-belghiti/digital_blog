## Purpose

This vault is for **developing opinions**.
Good engineers — senior engineers — have opinions about software, systems, and how to deliver business value. Not arbitrary preferences, but informed positions built from experience, reading, and deliberate thinking. This vault is where that thinking happens.

---

### Three Domains of Understanding

**Software Development**
- The actual work: features, solving bugs
- Reflecting on work done
- Organizing work effectively
- Improving workflow and tooling
- Developing opinions about *how software should be built*

**Systems Thinking**
Software is systems. But systems thinking extends far beyond code:
- Design patterns in software
- Feedback loops, emergence, leverage points (Donella Meadows)
- Mental models for decision-making (Farnam Street)
- Rhizomatic structures and non-hierarchical connections (Deleuze)
- Systems in society, biology, economics, organizations

Understanding systems *in general* enriches understanding of software systems *in particular*.

**Business & Value**
Code by itself does nothing. Software engineering is fundamentally about transforming business requirements into working systems:
- Recognizing business patterns and domain problems
- Understanding what stakeholders actually need (vs what they say)
- Translating requirements into technical decisions
- Measuring impact — how does this code create value?
- Communicating technical work in business terms

### How Knowledge Flows

```
sources/ (raw)  →  notes/ (atomic)  →  maps/ (connected)  →  opinions (formed)
     ↑                   ↑                    ↑                     ↓
 clippings          one idea            index notes           value proposition
 book notes         per note            cross-domain          (refined over time)
 lectures           your words
 conversations
 work experience
```

1. **Capture** — Clip articles, take notes from books/lectures/AI/work into `sources/`
2. **Process** — Extract atomic ideas into `notes/`, linking back to source
3. **Connect** — Build Maps of Content in `maps/` as patterns emerge
4. **Form** — Opinions crystallize from connected understanding
5. **Reflect** — Regularly revisit value proposition with new evidence

---

### Guiding Principles

- **Atomic notes**: One idea per note, in my own words
- **Link liberally**: Connections reveal structure — especially across domains
- **Tag by domain** (`#react`, `#laravel`, `#systems-thinking`, `#business`, `#philosophy`)
- **Process, don't hoard**: A source unread is noise
- **Opinions require foundation**: An opinion without supporting notes is just a preference
- **Code serves business**: Always ask "what problem does this solve?"
- **Value is demonstrated**: Collect evidence, not just claims
